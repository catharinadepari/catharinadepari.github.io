Phenomenal Mbah Maridjan

Mbah Maridjan was the cultural symbol of Mount Merapi. Despite his death during the 2010 eruption, his legacy remained. He gained popularity due to his refusal to leave the volcano during the 2006 eruption. The Sultan who was also the governor of the province of Yogyakarta Special Regions was perceived by the gate keeper as political leader rather than a Sultan. Ironically, he was appointed by the former Sultan to lead the spiritual rite of Labuhan Merapi. Mount Merapi is indeed, rich of mythologies and indigenous knowledge of disasters.

“The whole people in this village are my family. Evacuating for 2.5 months in barracks with them has united us. If I can choose, I prefer to live in my former house although today, I could physically and emotinoally become much closer to my neighbors. When we open our front doors, we can immediately see everyone. Those who see me would ask for instance, “Madam, are you on your way home?” In the past, we lived separately so we barely met one to another. Today, we become much closer and can talk to each other.”
From Interview with a local villager in Palemsari Sub-village in 2019 about social cohesion
