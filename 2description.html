What should we know about Mount Merapi?

The content of the website introduces you to key aspects influencing Mount Merapi's sustainabilty: environment, community, and the government.

Environment

Merapi’s eruption in 2010 with rapid onset and large magnitude had displaced almost 400,000 people and posed great challenges in evacuation processes (Mei, et al., 2013, p. 2). Despite these impacts, most villagers returned to their settlements and developed various activities including geotourism business in areas situated only 5 kms from the volcano’s crater (Ginting, 2020; Sagala, et al., 2012, p. 1; Davis, 2013).(Ginting, 2020)

Community

Eruption in 1006 had created a massive mobilization of the ancient society of Mataram Hindu from Central Java to East Java (Triyoga, 1991, p. 21).Influenced by Hindu-Buddha’s teachings from 8th to 16th A.D., the ancient community of Java perceived Merapi as a sacred place where their ancestral spirits and deities resided and the Sultan as the direct descendent of the God (Triyoga, 1991, p. 35).

Gate Keeper

As a royal servant appointed directly by Sultan , Mbah Maridjan was obliged to prepare and lead the court’s spiritual rite Labuhan Gunung on the slopes of Merapi. The aim of this rite is to maintain the harmonious relationship between the deities living in Mount Merapi, those in the South Sea, and the Sultanate.(Maarif, et al., 2012, p. 7).
